## Area Amateur Radio NETs

NETs (network operations) are organized on-air gatherings where amateur radio operators check in, practice communications, and share information. Below are the regular NETs in our area.

### Daily NETs

#### Morning Drive-Time NET
**Frequency**: 146.52 MHz (Simplex)
**Time**: Monday - Friday, 7:30 AM
**Net Control**: Rotating
**Format**: Informal check-ins, traffic, announcements
**Purpose**: Morning commuter net for local operators

---

#### Evening Roundtable
**Frequency**: 147.180 MHz (+600, PL 123.0)
**Time**: Every evening, 8:00 PM
**Net Control**: Rotating schedule
**Format**: Open discussion and elmering
**Purpose**: Social net, technical discussions, Q&A

---

### Weekly NETs

#### Sunday Morning Emergency NET
**Frequency**: 146.94 MHz (+600, PL 100.0)
**Time**: Every Sunday, 9:00 AM
**Net Control**: ARES/RACES team
**Format**: Formal check-ins, emergency preparedness
**Purpose**: Emergency communications training
**Notes**: All operators welcome, great for practice

---

#### Monday Tech NET
**Frequency**: 147.180 MHz (+600, PL 123.0)
**Time**: Every Monday, 7:30 PM
**Net Control**: Technical Committee members
**Format**: Technical topics and discussions
**Purpose**: Learn about equipment, modes, and techniques
**Notes**: Great for new operators

---

#### Tuesday Traffic NET
**Frequency**: 3.920 MHz (LSB)
**Time**: Every Tuesday, 8:00 PM local
**Net Control**: NTS Liaison
**Format**: National Traffic System formal message handling
**Purpose**: Practice traffic handling procedures
**Notes**: Part of regional traffic system

---

#### Wednesday HF Ragchew
**Frequency**: 7.250 MHz (LSB)
**Time**: Every Wednesday, 7:00 PM local
**Net Control**: Rotating
**Format**: Informal roundtable discussion
**Purpose**: HF operating practice, social
**Notes**: 40m alternative on 3.850 MHz if conditions poor

---

#### Thursday Digital NET
**Frequency**: 14.070 MHz (FT8/FT4)
**Time**: Every Thursday, 8:00 PM UTC
**Net Control**: Digital modes group
**Format**: FT8 check-ins, then roundtable on repeater
**Purpose**: Digital modes practice and discussion
**Notes**: WSJT-X required, info session on 147.180 after

---

#### Friday Simplex NET
**Frequency**: Starting on 146.52 MHz
**Time**: Every Friday, 7:00 PM
**Net Control**: Rotating
**Format**: Simplex operations, various frequencies
**Purpose**: Test portable and mobile setups
**Notes**: May QSY to other simplex frequencies

---

#### Saturday Morning Breakfast NET
**Frequency**: 147.300 MHz (+600, PL 88.5)
**Time**: Every Saturday, 8:00 AM
**Net Control**: Rotating
**Format**: Very informal, social
**Purpose**: Weekend greetings and planning
**Notes**: Often followed by coffee meetup

---

**Format**: Practice exchanges and procedures
**Purpose**: Prepare for contests
**Notes**: Check calendar for schedule

### NET Etiquette

**How to Check In**:
1. Listen first to understand the format
2. Wait for Net Control to call for check-ins
3. Give your callsign clearly (phonetics optional)
4. State if you have traffic or just checking in
5. Be brief and courteous

**General Guidelines**:
- Use proper operating procedures
- Keep transmissions concise
- No rag-chewing during formal nets
- Turn off CTCSS/PL squelch to hear all stations
- Stand by if you miss your turn
- Thank Net Control when you sign off
